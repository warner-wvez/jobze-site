The backstory

Why did I build this?

Started as ▾
Personal deal tracker
RECONv1 didn't originally start as a business tool. It was built to monitor promo deals at stores I regularly shop at. Before RECONv1, I regularly missed the promotional emails my favorite store would send to my inbox with 50,000+ unread emails, only to later be buried and never found. After successfully constructing the tool, I spent a week of using it to source my own deals. Then, the lightbulb hit: "Imagine if a small business had this tool."
Became ▾
Competitive intel engine
I pivoted from tracking two stores for their live promos and price changes, to creating a workflow that was capable of monitoring full inventories of stores in any radius, then comparing their live data side-by-side to extract insights on demand. That's when RECON became what it is now, a tool that tells a business owner exactly where they're winning (and losing) on prices.
Result ▾
24/7 Inventory Snapshots
From the first HTTP request to a fully working pipeline with built-in analysis in 2.5 weeks. One click now pulls 1,000+ products from two stores (or more), normalizes the data to match each other, generates stable SKUs, archives everything to Drive and Supabase, stores the data, and writes a competitive intelligence report. No manual work.
Biggest lesson ▾
Data storage & Batching
I knew early on after the pivot I wanted some kind of pattern learning built into the system. I wanted the tool to get smarter over time and learn how well a business was truly operating compared to its nearby competitors. But I didn't know how to store thousands of product records efficiently. That's when I discovered Supabase and taught myself about batching, vector embeddings, and how to build a system that compounds its own knowledge with every run.

Step by step

How it works

01Trigger1 node▾
One click starts everything. All three data pulls fire at the same time.
02Live Data Collection3 nodes▾
Pulls every product from both stores at once. The competitor store uses a headless browser so it looks like a real person browsing. The client store is a direct API call.
03Normalize3 nodes▾
Both stores send data in completely different formats. This step translates both into one matching format so they can be compared side-by-side.
04Merge & Fingerprint2 nodes▾
Combines all products into one list. Each product gets a permanent ID. Same product next week gets the same ID. That's how prices get tracked over time.
05Route & Archive7 nodes▾
Splits data back into competitor vs. client. Saves both to Google Sheets, converts to Excel, and uploads timestamped backups to Google Drive.
06AI Memory5 nodes▾
Every product is saved to a database and converted into AI-readable format. This is how the system remembers past runs and learns patterns over time.
07AI Intelligence6 nodes▾
An AI reads all 1,100+ products and writes a report: where you're cheaper, what the competitor is out of, and what to do about it.

RECON started as a way to track my own deals and became a full competitive intelligence engine in 2-3 weeks. Every run builds on the last. The system's memory compounds over time. Point it at any two retail endpoints and it works.
